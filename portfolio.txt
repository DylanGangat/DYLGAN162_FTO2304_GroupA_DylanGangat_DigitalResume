Creating a 90's-themed frontend developer portfolio website sounds like a fun and unique project! To help you get started, here are some suggestions for sections, color schemes, animations, and resources:

Sections for your portfolio website:
1. Header: Introduce yourself with a catchy headline or slogan.
2. About: Provide a brief overview of your background, skills, and expertise.
3. Work/Projects: Showcase your best projects with descriptions, screenshots, and links.
4. Skills: List your technical skills and proficiencies.
5. Experience: Highlight your relevant work experience, internships, or freelance projects.
6. Testimonials: Include positive feedback or testimonials from clients or colleagues.
7. Contact: Provide contact information and a contact form for potential clients or employers.

Color scheme:
For a 90's theme, you can consider using vibrant and bold colors commonly seen during that era. Here are a few popular options:
- Neon: Bright neon colors like electric blue, hot pink, and lime green.
- Pastels: Soft and muted pastel shades like lavender, mint green, and baby blue.
- Gradients: Apply gradient effects using contrasting or complementary colors.

Animations:
To maintain a minimalistic approach while incorporating subtle 90's-inspired animations, you can consider the following ideas:
- Parallax scrolling: Create a sense of depth by moving background and foreground elements at different speeds as the user scrolls.
- Hover effects: Apply simple animations or transitions to elements when users hover over them, such as changing colors or scaling the elements.
- Animated loaders: Use animated loaders or progress bars to enhance user experience while content is loading.
- Animated backgrounds: Consider using animated GIFs or CSS animations to add dynamic backgrounds to certain sections.
